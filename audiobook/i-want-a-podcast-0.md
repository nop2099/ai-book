# Episode 0: What Is This?

The trailer. Two understudies for Vic and Sam describe the podcast. Dry mix.

## Voice Design Notes

### Cast

| Tag | Voice | Draft Voice | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| VIC-U | — | Moira (Irish) | Vic's understudy. Confident, talks too much. |
| SAM-U | — | Daniel | Sam's understudy. Patient, asks the right question. |

### Assembly overrides

- **Dry mix.** No effects.

## Script

[VIC-U]
It's a podcast about wanting a podcast.

[SAM-U]
That's circular.

[VIC-U]
It's a strange loop. You say what you want. An AI builds it. You push back when it's wrong. By the end you have the thing AND you understand why it works. Then you teach someone else with one sentence.

[SAM-U]
What's the sentence?

[VIC-U]
"I want a podcast."

[SAM-U]
That's the name of the show.

[VIC-U]
That's the name, the pilot, the format, and the prompt. Four things. One sentence.

[SAM-U]
So what happens in it?

[VIC-U]
A guy named Rich opens an AI agent in an empty folder and says four words. The agent asks about his life. He talks about baking with Mike. The agent writes a script, generates audio, deploys it to a website with a password. Rich listens on his phone.

[SAM-U]
That's it?

[VIC-U]
That's the first ten minutes. Then he says "but if someone looks at the source code, can they find the audio file?" And the agent says yes. And Rich says "that's not good enough." And four corrections later the filenames are encrypted and Rich can explain why.

[SAM-U]
He learned encryption from baking?

[VIC-U]
He learned encryption from pushing back. The baking was the excuse to start. The security thread was the spine. The understanding came from the corrections, not from a textbook.

[SAM-U]
Who are the voices?

[VIC-U]
Seven of them. Rich is the user. The octopus is the command-line agent — full reach, touches everything. The crab is the desktop agent — has hands but lives in a sandbox. The parrot is the free web version — smart but can't touch files. There's an error voice — a robot that shows up when things break. And the stage — she knows how the story ends.

[SAM-U]
And you?

[VIC-U]
I'm the understudy. For a character called Vic. Apparently she has strong opinions about spreadsheets.

[SAM-U]
I'm the understudy for Sam. Apparently he listens.

[VIC-U]
That tracks.

[SAM-U]
What's the format?

[VIC-U]
First person. Rich lives it. The octopus builds it. The parrot watches from his shoulder and says "I can do that too, but you'd have to carry the file." The crab says "I can't see that folder" and then can. The stage explains what just happened and why it mattered.

[SAM-U]
How long is it?

[VIC-U]
Two parts. Part one is the build — thirty-five minutes. Part two is what you built — connectors, spreadsheets, scheduling, and a postscript where a senior enterprise architect looks at what Rich made and says "that's a complete system architecture."

[SAM-U]
Rich built an enterprise architecture?

[VIC-U]
By accident. He just wanted to listen to a podcast on his phone.

[SAM-U]
And this whole thing was made with the tools it describes?

[VIC-U]
Seven macOS voices. A Python script. A cache that skips unchanged lines. f-f-mpeg for the conversion. Deployed with one command. No G.P.U. No studio. No recording. The technology is thirty years old. The ability to assemble it with a conversation is six months old.

[SAM-U]
So how do I start?

[VIC-U]
Open your agent. Say "I want a podcast." Then let the conversation happen. Push back when something tastes wrong.

[SAM-U]
That's the show?

[VIC-U]
That's the show. Read the book and let's build.
